## About

I am a pediatric emergency physician, which means I spend a lot of time seeing what happens when care arrives late.

Most of my work now starts with one question: how can data and AI help us reach people sooner—before a missed screening, a preventable complication, or a long drive becomes a crisis?

In Kentucky, I am working on several practical pieces of that problem:

- KY-AHEAD, a University of Kentucky collaboration with Kentucky CHFS focused on cancer-screening gaps
- rural retinal-screening work aimed at finding important health risks earlier and connecting patients to follow-up care
- the UK AI Incubator and university-wide AI literacy work, where clinicians, educators, students, and staff can test these tools without pretending they are magic
- research on clinical reasoning and assessment

The model is usually the easy part. The hard part is the network around it: data that can move, workflows people can use, clear ownership, and a real next step for the patient.

My long-term interest is shared, EMR-agnostic infrastructure that can find a useful signal, connect someone to care, and close the loop.

If your group is wrestling with the same question, bring me into the room. I would love to compare notes.

Views are my own.
