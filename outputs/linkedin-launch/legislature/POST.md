# LinkedIn post - Kentucky General Assembly / KY-AHEAD

## Post copy

At the General Assembly last September, I talked about why healthcare AI in Kentucky needs Kentucky data. The concrete example was cancer screening: use state data to identify Kentuckians who may be overdue for colorectal, cervical, or lung cancer screening, reach out to them, help them understand the next step, and make it easier to get scheduled. That kind of pathway depends on the state and the university doing work that neither could do alone.

At the time, this was a proposal. Since then, the KY-AHEAD prototype has become functional and a contract with Kentucky CHFS is now in place. The hard part is not finished. We still have to connect the data, the outreach, and the clinical workflow in a way that is secure and actually useful to patients and care teams. But the work is moving, and I think this is a good example of what a real state-university partnership can make possible.
