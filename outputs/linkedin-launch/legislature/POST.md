# LinkedIn post - Kentucky General Assembly / KY-AHEAD

## Post copy

Kentucky is building its health intelligence layer one care pathway at a time. In this 2025 exchange with the General Assembly, I described one of those pathways: use Kentucky data to identify people who may be overdue for cancer screening, reach them, answer their questions, and make it easier to get scheduled. Senator Amanda Mays Bledsoe ended the discussion by saying, "Whatever we need to do on our side, we want to be a good partner."

That partnership is now underway. KY-AHEAD has a functional prototype, and a state-university partnership with the Office of Data Analytics at Kentucky CHFS is underway. It is one project, not the whole system. But it demonstrates the operating model: state data, university technical capacity, and patient-facing services connected around getting people into care earlier. We are building this in Kentucky.
